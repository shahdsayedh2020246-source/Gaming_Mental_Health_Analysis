## 📖 The Tableau Story: The Hidden Cost of Gaming
An interactive, narrative-driven presentation split into **5 data-backed stories**:

### 🎯 Story 1: 6 Hours a Day
> **Subtitle:** *6 hours a day — more than most people spend at work.*
![Story 1](Story%201%20Tableau.png)
* **Key Insight:** Establishes the baseline exposure. While 65% of the player base identified as male, the data reveals that **all genders average above 5.5 hours/day**. Furthermore, across all 4 platforms (Console, PC, Multi-platform, Mobile), usage consistently hovers **above 6 hours/day**, proving that the issue is the duration, not the platform itself.

### 💰 Story 2: Every Hour Has a Price
> **Subtitle:** *Every extra hour costs money — and your mood decides how much.*
![Story 2](Story%202%20Tableau.png)
* **Key Insight:** Links behavioral spending with psychological well-being. Anxious and depressed players logged the highest lifetime spend at **$10.2K**, which is **3x more** than excited players ($3.1K). This establishes a clear pattern: very high spenders game an average of **10.5 hours/day**, demonstrating that financial output and time commitment scale together tightly with negative emotional states.

### 🫁 Story 3: Their Bodies Are Paying Too
> **Subtitle:** *It's not just money. Sleep, health, and productivity are all taking a hit.*
![Story 3](Story%203%20Tableau.png)
* **Key Insight:** Explores academic and physical fallout. Academic performance shows a stark inverse relationship with gaming hours: **Failing students game 8.5 hours/day**, whereas **Excellent students game only 3.9 hours/day** (a massive 4.5-hour daily gap). Physically, high-risk players experience a high volume of neck, back, and eye strains, sleeping only **4.5 hours/night** compared to low-exposure players who get a healthier **6.7 hours**.

### 🛑 Story 4: 14.2% Can't Stop
> **Subtitle:** *For 1 in 7 players, gaming stopped being a hobby.*
![Story 4](Story%204%20Tableau%20%281%29.png)
* **Key Insight:** Isolates clinical addiction thresholds. Severe addiction risks cause immediate real-world consequences: severe risk profiles spend only **3.2 hours/week face-to-face** with people compared to **10.2 hours** for low-risk individuals, leading to a severe social isolation score of **6.5**. Statistically, once a player crosses the average screen-time threshold, the likelihood of jumping into a **Severe Risk** classification instantly spikes to **61%**.

### 🛣️ Story 5: the Way Out
> **Subtitle:** *The patterns are clear. Small changes make a big difference.*
![Story 5](Story%205%20Tableau.png)
* **Key Insight:** Converts analytical findings into actionable therapeutic solutions across 4 distinct pillars:
  1. **Sleep & Physical Health:** Enforcing a rule of at least 7 hours of sleep and taking screen breaks using the 20-20-20 rule.
  2. **Addiction Risk:** Capping gaming time at a critical threshold of **3.5 hours/day** and utilizing platform time limits.
  3. **Social Life:** Reclaiming real-world connection by committing to at least **10 hours/week face-to-face** to actively counter isolation.
  4. **Spending Habits:** Mitigating impulsive purchasing by implementing a **24-hour waiting period** before any in-game transaction and setting strict banking limits.
